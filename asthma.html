Asthma

Defination

Asthma is one of the diseases that affect the bronchial tubes that deliver air to and from the lungs, and it usually appears in children with the possibility of its appearance at any other age.Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition characterized by inflammation and narrowing of the airways, which can cause difficulty breathing, wheezing, and coughing

Reasons

Exposure to cold air. Exposure to certain allergens, such as: mold, animal dander, tree pollen, grass, and some types of medicines. The exact causes of asthma are not fully understood, but it is believed to be caused by a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Some common triggers of asthma include: 1. Allergens: Allergens such as pollen, dust mites, and animal dander can trigger asthma symptoms in some people. 2. Irritants: Irritants such as smoke, air pollution, and strong odors can also trigger asthma symptoms. 3. Respiratory infections: Respiratory infections such as the common cold or flu can trigger asthma symptoms in some people. 4. Exercise: Exercise-induced asthma is a type of asthma triggered by physical activity. 5. Emotional stress: Emotional stress can also trigger asthma symptoms in some people. 6. Occupational exposure: Some people may develop occupational asthma as a result of exposure to certain chemicals or substances in the workplace.

Treatment

Anti-inflammatories It includes inhaled steroids, which reduce inflammation in the area and reduce the patient's risk of severe seizures. The use of this form of treatment helps to reduce the side effects associated with oral steroids. bronchodilators; This type of treatment works to expand the bronchi, which relieves the condition within seconds to a few minutes and for a period of time ranging from 4-12 hours. -leukotriene levels. -Immunotherapy. -Vital medicines. -Prevention of asthma in children. There is no cure for asthma, but symptoms can be managed with proper treatment. Treatment for asthma typically involves a combination of medications and lifestyle changes. Medications used to treat asthma may include inhaled bronchodilators, inhaled corticosteroids, and leukotriene modifiers. Lifestyle changes that can help manage asthma symptoms include avoiding triggers, maintaining a healthy weight, and exercising regularly (under the guidance of a healthcare provider). In addition, in severe cases of asthma, immunotherapy (allergy shots) or biologic medications may be used to help reduce inflammation in the airways.
