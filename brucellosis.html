Brucellosis

Defination

It is a bacterial disease caused by different types of brucellosis bacteria, and it is transmitted by animals directly or indirectly, and often affects people working in the livestock sector, as the consumption of milk and cheese made from milk (fresh cheese) is the main source of infection in humans, and the transmission of Human-to-human transmission is very rare, and brucellosis remains a problem globally. Because it is the most common bacterial infection from animals to humans worldwide, animals may carry the bacteria without showing any symptoms.Brucellosis, also known as undulant fever, is a bacterial infection that is typically spread to humans by contact with infected animals or consumption of contaminated animal products.

Reasons

It is caused by infection with the infectious brucellosis bacteria. Ways of transmission: Drinking unpasteurized milk or its products, or eating undercooked meat from infected animals. Direct contact with infected animals or their secretions (tissue, blood, urine, vaginal secretions, aborted fetuses, especially the placentas) through the presence of cracks in the skin. The symptoms of brucellosis can vary widely from person to person, but may include fever, chills, sweats, fatigue, headache, muscle and joint pain, and loss of appetite. In some cases, brucellosis can lead to more serious complications, such as inflammation of the heart or brain, arthritis, or chronic fatigue syndrome. Brucellosis is typically diagnosed through blood tests that detect the presence of antibodies to the Brucella bacteria. In some cases, other tests, such as bone marrow culture or spinal fluid analysis, may be necessary to confirm the diagnosis

Types

1-Brucella maltese (melitensis): It is found in sheep, and it is the most widespread and the most severe. 2-Brucellosis swine (Swiss): It affects those in contact with the animal and is considered highly effective. 3-Brucella abortus (Abortus): It is found in cows, and is considered to be of medium severity. 4-Canis brucellosis (Canis): affects workers with dogs and is of moderate severity. Treatment

Treatment aims to relieve symptoms and prevent complications and depends on the timing and severity of the disease. It may take a few weeks to several months to recover from it, by taking antibiotics for at least six weeks. Brucellosis is typically treated with a combination of antibiotics, such as doxycycline and rifampin, for a period of several weeks to several months. In some cases, other antibiotics may be used, depending on the severity of the infection and the individual's medical history. It is important to complete the entire course of antibiotics, even if symptoms improve, to prevent the infection from recurring. The best way to prevent brucellosis is to avoid contact with infected animals or animal products. This includes wearing protective clothing when handling animals, consuming only pasteurized dairy products, and avoiding consumption of meat from infected animals. In addition, vaccination may be recommended for individuals at high risk of exposure to brucellosis. Overall, brucellosis is a serious bacterial infection that requires prompt diagnosis and treatment. By taking steps to prevent infection, such as avoiding contact with infected animals and consuming only pasteurized dairy products, it is possible to reduce the risk of contracting the disease.
